    Summary measures of health disparities by Race/Ethnicity — 2010

    • The best group rate for this objective, 86.3%, was attained by the White only, not Hispanic or Latino population.
    • The worst group rate for this objective, 56%, was attained by the American Indian or Alaska Native only population.
    • The absolute (or range) difference between the best and worst group rates was 30.3.
    • The best group rate was 1.542 times the worst group rate.
    • The best group rate was 1.161 times the average rate for all worse-off groups, 74.3%.

    Detailed measures of health disparities by Race/Ethnicity — 2010

    The White only, not Hispanic or Latino population achieved the best group rate for this objective, 86.3%.

    The best group rate was:

    • 1.024 times the rate among persons identifying with 2 or more races.
    • 1.040 times the rate for the Asian only population.
    • 1.087 times the rate for the Black or African American only, not Hispanic or Latino population.
    • 1.141 times the rate for the Native Hawaiian or Other Pacific Islander only population.
    • 1.270 times the rate for the Hispanic or Latino population.
    • 1.542 times the rate for the American Indian or Alaska Native only population.
